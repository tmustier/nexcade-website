About Team

We spent four years watching forwarders re-key data that already existed.

Now we're fixing it.

The problem we saw

At Sedna, we built email tools for supply chain operators. We gave them back an hour a day. But we kept seeing the same thing: pricing teams manually copying data from emails into spreadsheets, cross-referencing rate cards, chasing suppliers across time zones, and building quotes line by line.

The data was there. It was just trapped—in inboxes, in spreadsheets, in people's heads. And the tools that existed were either too broad to go deep on quoting, or too rigid to handle the chaos of real freight forwarding.

Why now

Pre-2023, automating this kind of work was uneconomic. Every supplier had different formats. Every customer had different requirements. Every lane had its quirks. You'd need custom extraction modules for each one—the math didn't work.

LLMs changed that. Not because they're magic, but because they can handle the messiness that ruled out automation before. Unstructured emails. Inconsistent rate sheets. The fuzzy edges that humans navigate without thinking.

We founded Nexcade in early 2025 to build what's now possible.

What we believe

Freight forwarding is a data business that doesn't know it yet. Forwarders sit on years of pricing history, supplier relationships, and customer preferences—but it's scattered and inaccessible.

We think the companies that capture and use their data will win. Not by replacing their teams, but by giving them tools that match the complexity of the work.

We're starting with quoting because it's high-value, high-frequency, and close to the money. But quoting is just the wedge.
